The Root Cause Behind Your Dog’s Excessive Itching & Scratching

When your dog is exposed to these allergens, their body reacts by producing an antibody called immunoglobulin E (IgE).

IgE triggers the release of histamine and other chemicals in the body.

On one hand, this is good…

These chemicals cause your dog’s blood vessels to widen and become more permeable.

This allows white blood cells and other immune cells to migrate to the site of an injury or infection, where they can fight off any foreign invaders.

But on the other hand…

Histamine can also trigger allergy symptoms such as itching, swelling, and inflammation when the immune system overreacts to harmless substances like pollen.

In simple terms, this causes the excessive itching & scratching your dog is suffering from.

But there’s one problem though…

Seasonal allergies in dogs don't just disappear once spring is over.

The allergens that trigger the reaction can continue to be present in the environment for an extended period.

For example, grass and weed pollen persist well into the summer and fall months.

But it gets worse…

Repeated exposure to allergens can cause the immune system to become sensitized…

Meaning that the body overreacts to even small amounts of the allergen.

This can lead to long-term allergy symptoms that persist even after the initial trigger has been removed…

Leaving your dog itching & scratching themselves raw without a break…

Which can lead to even more problems…

Like developing secondary bacterial or yeast infections due to their weakened immune system and constant scratching and itching.
